CHINESE BEEF &
BROCCOLI

Bring classic Chinese takeout home and make it
even better with this easy beef and broccoli stir
fry. Tender steak and crisp broccoli are coated
in a savory, slightly sweet sauce. It all comes
together in one pan and under 30 minutes for
the ultimate weeknight dinner.

PREP TIME: 10 MINUTES                        COOK TIME: 20 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 30 MINUTES                     YIELDS: 4


INGREDIENTS

FOR THE STEAK:

  • 1 pound flank, NY strip, or ribeye
    steak, thinly sliced against the
    grain
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

FOR THE VEGETABLE STIR FRY:

  • 1 pound broccoli florets
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons neutral oil, as needed
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste

FOR THE SAUCE:

  • 1/3 cup low sodium beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • pinch of cayenne powder

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Marinate the steak: In a medium bowl, toss the sliced steak with
    cornstarch, dark soy sauce, baking soda, white pepper, and salt.
    Set aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In another bowl or measuring cup, whisk beef
    broth, low sodium soy sauce, brown sugar, oyster sauce, rice
    vinegar, dark soy sauce, cornstarch, white pepper, and cayenne
    powder together until smooth. Set aside.
  3. Cook the broccoli: Heat 1 tablespoon neutral oil in a large pan or
    wok over medium heat. Add broccoli and stir-fry briefly. Add a
    splash of water, cover, and steam for 2-3 minutes until broccoli is
    tender-crisp. Remove broccoli and set aside.
  4. Cook the steak: Over medium-high heat, heat the wok or pan until
    it’s very hot, then add 1 1/2 to 2 tablespoons neutral oil. Add the
    marinated steak and stir-fry until just cooked through and seared,
    about 2-3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  5. Sauté aromatics: Lower the heat to medium-low. Add sesame oil
    and 1/2 tablespoon neutral oil to the pan. Add garlic and ginger,
    sauté gently until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  6. Combine: Return broccoli and steak to the pan. Pour in the sauce
    and toss everything together until the sauce thickens and coats
    evenly, about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with rice or noodles.
